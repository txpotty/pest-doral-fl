Termites

Termites are silent destroyers, causing billions of dollars in damage annually. They feed on wood and can compromise the structural integrity of your home or business without you even knowing. Detecting termites early and implementing an effective termite treatment is critical to preventing extensive and costly repairs. Termites are often difficult to detect in the early stages of an infestation because they live and work within the wooden structures they are consuming. Signs of a termite infestation can be subtle, such as mud tubes on foundations, discarded wings near windowsills, or hollow-sounding wood. Doral’s humid climate is particularly conducive to termite activity, making property owners in the area especially vulnerable to infestations. Different species of termites exist in Florida, including subterranean termites and drywood termites, each requiring different identification and treatment methods. Subterranean termites, for example, live in underground colonies and build mud tubes to travel between their nest and food source. Drywood termites, on the other hand, infest dry wood and do not require contact with the soil. Our certified technicians are experts in identifying the type of termite infesting your property and assessing the extent of the damage. A thorough inspection is the first step in developing an effective termite treatment plan. We utilize advanced techniques and technologies for termite detection, such as infrared cameras and moisture meters, to locate hidden colonies within walls and other structures. Based on our assessment, we will recommend the most appropriate treatment method, which may include liquid termiticides applied to the soil around the foundation, baiting systems, or localized treatments for drywood termites. Our goal is to eliminate the existing termite colony and create a protective barrier to prevent future infestations. We also advise on preventative measures, such as eliminating moisture sources near the foundation, storing wood away from the house, and ensuring proper ventilation in crawl spaces.
