Understanding Squirrel Behavior and Common Problems

Gaining insight into squirrel behavior helps in effectively managing infestations. Gray squirrels, the most common species found in Doral, are highly adaptable and opportunistic. They are known for their strong gnawing instincts, essential for keeping their teeth sharp and accessing food. This gnawing behavior is often the source of significant property damage, as they may chew on wood, plastic, and even electrical wiring. Their natural drive to gather and store food leads them to search for safe and hidden locations, which unfortunately often includes attics, walls, and crawl spaces within human structures. Understanding these fundamental aspects of their behavior allows us to anticipate their actions and implement more effective control and prevention strategies. Squirrels are particularly active during the morning and late afternoon, times when they are most likely to be observed entering or exiting a building. Their ability to jump and climb makes them adept at navigating trees and structures, further complicating removal efforts. Their breeding season, typically occurring twice a year, can lead to increased activity and the presence of young, requiring more specialized removal approaches to ensure the safety of both the animals and the inhabitants of the property. The presence of young squirrels dictates a more cautious approach to removal, often requiring waiting until the young are old enough to survive independently before trapping and relocating the mother. This humane consideration is a core principle of our services. If you're also dealing with animals like raccoons, our raccoon removal services are available. Signs You Need Squirrel Removal

Being able to recognize the early signs of a squirrel infestation can save you from more significant problems down the line. Prompt detection and intervention are key to minimizing damage and disruption. Keep an eye and ear out for these common indicators that suggest squirrels have taken up residence in your Doral home or business.

Noises in the Attic or Walls

One of the most frequent signs of squirrels is the presence of unexplained noises coming from your attic, walls, or ceiling. These sounds are often described as scratching, scurrying, chattering, or the rolling of nuts. Squirrels are generally more active during the day, so if you hear these noises during daylight hours, it's a strong indication of a squirrel infestation. The sounds may be particularly noticeable in the early morning or late afternoon as they move about. Similar noises might indicate a need for our rodent control services.

Visible Damage to Property

Squirrels are notorious for their gnawing habits, and this can lead to visible damage to your property. Look for chewed wood around eaves, vents, or fascia boards. They may also damage electrical wiring, increasing the risk of fire. Inside, you might find chewed insulation, damaged ductwork, or gnaw marks on wooden beams. Ripped screens and damaged soffits are also common indicators of squirrel activity. This sort of damage requires professional assessment and remediation to prevent further issues. Addressing damage promptly is crucial for maintaining the structural integrity of your property.

Nesting Materials and Droppings

Finding nesting materials or droppings in areas where they shouldn't be is another clear sign of a squirrel problem. Squirrels often build nests in attics, chimneys, or wall cavities using insulation, leaves, and other debris. Their droppings are small and pellet-shaped and can accumulate in areas where they are nesting or frequently travel. The presence of these materials not only confirms an infestation but also highlights potential health risks associated with animal waste. Proper cleanup and sanitation are essential after removal. The odors associated with droppings can also attract other pests, creating additional problems for homeowners. Eliminating these biological hazards is a crucial step in restoring a healthy environment.

Observed Squirrel Activity

Simply seeing squirrels frequently entering or exiting your home or business is a direct indication of an infestation. They may be seen using tree branches to access the roof or climbing up walls. Observing their behavior and entry points can provide valuable information for developing a removal plan. Pay attention to the times of day they are most active and the routes they are taking. Their consistent presence suggests they have established a territory on or within your property. Sometimes, squirrels might be seen carrying nesting materials into your home. This is a clear sign they are preparing to establish a den or raise young within the structure. Recognizing these subtle behaviors can help you identify an infestation early on. Other signs of activity might involve finding caches of nuts hidden in unusual places around your property. This food storage behavior is instinctive for squirrels and can sometimes lead them into problematic areas.

Potential Health Risks Associated with Squirrels

While not as commonly associated with disease transmission as some other pests, squirrels can still pose potential health risks to humans. Their presence in living spaces increases the likelihood of exposure to pathogens through droppings, urine, or bites. It is important to approach any interaction with wild animals with caution and to seek professional help for removal to minimize these risks. Awareness of these potential hazards underscores the importance of professional removal and sanitation. Beyond direct contact, the parasites they carry can also present health concerns. For comprehensive pest control, including ant control or cockroach control, we have solutions.

Histoplasmosis

One of the primary health concerns associated with squirrel droppings is the risk of contracting histoplasmosis. This respiratory illness is caused by a fungus that grows in soil contaminated with animal droppings, including those of squirrels. When the spores of this fungus are inhaled, they can cause flu-like symptoms, and in more severe cases, lead to serious lung infections. Activities that disturb areas with accumulated droppings, such as cleaning out an attic or crawl space without proper protective gear, increase the risk of exposure. Professional cleanup includes safety protocols to mitigate this risk. The fungus thrives in warm, moist environments, making areas like attics particularly susceptible to its growth. Proper ventilation and moisture control within your property are also important preventative measures against this fungal growth. For issues with spiders or silverfish, we can assist.

Squirrel Bites and Scratches

While squirrels generally avoid human interaction, cornered or frightened animals may bite or scratch in self-defense. Squirrel bites and scratches, while relatively uncommon, can transmit diseases such as rabies, although this is rare. Any bite or scratch from a wild animal should be thoroughly cleaned and medical attention sought to assess the risk and receive necessary treatment. It's crucial to avoid attempting to handle or remove squirrels yourself to prevent these potential exposures. Even a seemingly minor scratch can become infected if not properly cared for. Teaching children to never approach or feed wild animals is an important part of preventing these incidents. If you are bitten or scratched, observing the animal's behavior from a safe distance can sometimes provide clues about its health status, but it is always best to err on the side of caution and seek medical advice.

Parasites

Squirrels can carry various parasites, including fleas, ticks, and mites, which can infest your home and pose a risk to your family and pets. These parasites can transmit diseases such as Lyme disease (from ticks) or cause skin irritations and allergic reactions. An infestation of parasites following squirrel removal may require additional flea treatment or tick control services. Addressing the squirrel infestation promptly helps to eliminate the source of these parasites and prevent their spread within your home. Ensuring your pets are on preventative medication for fleas and ticks is also a good practice, especially if you suspect wildlife has been on your property. Long-Term Prevention Strategies

Effective squirrel removal is not just about getting rid of the current inhabitants; it's also about implementing strategies to prevent future problems. Our technicians will provide recommendations and implement measures to make your property less attractive and accessible to squirrels, offering long-term protection against re-infestation. Trimming Tree Branches

Squirrels are excellent climbers and often use tree branches to gain access to roofs and other parts of your home. Trimming branches that hang over or touch your roof can significantly reduce their ability to reach your property. We recommend maintaining a distance of at least 6-8 feet between tree branches and your roofline. Regular tree maintenance not only helps with squirrel prevention but also protects your roof from leaf debris and potential damage during storms. Consider the type of trees on your property and their growth patterns when planning your trimming schedule. Utilizing professional arborist services can ensure trees are trimmed safely and effectively for both tree health and pest prevention. Securing Garbage Cans and Food Sources

Squirrels are attracted to accessible food sources. Ensuring your garbage cans have tight-fitting lids and are stored securely can prevent squirrels from foraging for food on your property. Avoid leaving pet food or birdseed outside, as these can also attract squirrels and other wildlife. Storing birdseed in sealed containers is particularly important. Consider using wildlife-resistant garbage cans if squirrels or other animals are a persistent problem in your area. Composting areas should also be managed carefully to avoid attracting unwanted visitors. Even seemingly insignificant food scraps can be a strong attractant for foraging squirrels, making proper waste management critical. Controlling potential food sources is a fundamental step in any effective wildlife prevention program.

Repairing and Sealing entry points

As mentioned earlier, repairing and sealing any potential entry points is crucial for long-term prevention. Regularly inspect your home for any cracks, gaps, or damaged areas in the roof, eaves, vents, and foundation. Repair any damage promptly to prevent squirrels from gaining access. Installing chimney caps and securing attic vents with sturdy mesh are particularly effective measures. Consider using materials that are resistant to gnawing, such as metal mesh, for sealing openings. A thorough annual inspection of your home's exterior can help identify potential vulnerabilities before they become significant problems. Proactive maintenance is always a wise investment when it comes to preventing future pest and wildlife issues. Don't forget to check around plumbing and electrical entries as well, as these can sometimes offer small, overlooked access points.
